# Prenatal Depression Research Toolkit

**Scope:** prenatal depression, antenatal depression, antepartum depression, depressive symptoms during pregnancy, and pregnancy-onset major depressive disorder.  
**Adjacent terms included when useful:** perinatal depression, peripartum depression, maternal mental health, postpartum depression, anxiety, trauma, bipolar disorder, sleep, nutrition, fetal and child outcomes.  
**Last updated:** 2026-05-09

> Practical note: No toolkit can literally list every journal in the world that may publish one relevant article. This document is designed as a high-yield, cross-disciplinary map of the journals, topic positions, keywords, and reusable search templates most likely to retrieve clinically and academically relevant work on prenatal depression.

---

## 1. Terminology Map

| Concept | Preferred search terms | Synonyms / variants | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prenatal depression | prenatal depression | depression during pregnancy, pregnancy depression, depression in pregnancy | Common in North American literature. |
| Antenatal depression | antenatal depression | antenatal depressive symptoms, antenatal maternal depression | Common in UK, Europe, Australia, and global health literature. |
| Antepartum depression | antepartum depression | antepartum depressive symptoms | Often used in obstetric literature. |
| Perinatal depression | perinatal depression | perinatal depressive symptoms, perinatal mood disorder | Broader than prenatal; includes pregnancy and postpartum. Useful for broader discovery, but filter for pregnancy-specific studies. |
| Peripartum depression | peripartum depression | major depressive episode with peripartum onset | DSM-related wording. May include onset during pregnancy or soon after delivery. |
| Maternal depression | maternal depression | maternal depressive symptoms | Broad; can include postpartum and child-age studies. Combine with pregnancy terms. |
| Depression diagnosis | major depressive disorder | MDD, depressive disorder, mood disorder, clinical depression | Use for diagnostic studies and pharmacotherapy. |
| Screening-positive depression | depressive symptoms | EPDS-positive, PHQ-9-positive, elevated depressive symptoms | Often used when studies use questionnaire cutoffs rather than clinical diagnosis. |
| High-risk pregnancy mental health | depression in high-risk pregnancy | obstetric complications, preeclampsia, gestational diabetes, hyperemesis, threatened preterm birth | Useful for obstetric comorbidity. |
| Exclusion terms | postpartum-only, postnatal-only | puerperal, after delivery, lactation-only | Do not overuse exclusion terms because many perinatal studies include both pregnancy and postpartum data. |

## 8. Search Refinement Strategy

### 8.1 Start broad, then split by question

1. Begin with phrase terms: `"prenatal depression" OR "antenatal depression" OR "depression during pregnancy"`.
2. Add one domain: screening, nutrition, inflammation, social determinants, treatment, infant outcomes.
3. Add one study design: cohort, randomized, systematic review, meta-analysis, qualitative, validation.
4. Add one population if needed: adolescent pregnancy, immigrant women, high-risk pregnancy, gestational diabetes, Taiwan, LMICs.
5. Check whether results are pregnancy-specific or postpartum-only.

### 8.2 Avoid missing important papers

Use both North American and UK/Commonwealth terminology:

```text
"prenatal depression" OR "antenatal depression" OR "antepartum depression"
```

Include broader perinatal terms when exploring:

```text
"perinatal depression" AND pregnancy
```

Use instrument terms:

```text
EPDS OR "Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale" OR PHQ-9
```

### 8.3 Avoid drowning in postpartum-only literature

Use title-only exclusion only when necessary:

```text
NOT (postpartum[ti] OR postnatal[ti] OR puerperal[ti])
```

Do not use broad `NOT postpartum[tiab]` at the first stage, because many relevant perinatal studies include both prenatal and postpartum data.

### 8.4 Use proximity search in PubMed when phrases vary

Example:

```text
"depression pregnancy"[tiab:~3]
```

```text
"maternal depression pregnancy"[tiab:~5]
```

Do not combine PubMed proximity searching with truncation inside the quoted proximity phrase.

## 14. Source Notes

- PubMed supports field tags such as `[tiab]`, `[Mesh]`, publication type tags, Boolean operators, and proximity searching in title/title-abstract fields.
- PubMed proximity searching can be useful for flexible phrases such as `"depression pregnancy"[tiab:~3]`, but it should not be combined with truncation inside the proximity phrase.
- Perinatal depression is broader than prenatal depression; it commonly covers pregnancy and postpartum periods. For prenatal-only searches, use pregnancy-specific terms and check abstracts manually.
- Nutritional studies on perinatal depression often focus on vitamin D, folate, iron, selenium, zinc, and fatty acids; findings vary by study quality and design.
- Clinical screening and prevention literature often includes both pregnant and postpartum populations. Extract pregnancy-specific subgroup data whenever possible.

## 15. Minimal Workflow for a New Review

1. Run the PubMed master query.
2. Run phrase-focused queries in Google Scholar and Scopus.
3. Split results into five folders: screening, risk factors, biology, interventions, outcomes.
4. Search `Archives of Women’s Mental Health`, `Journal of Affective Disorders`, `BMC Pregnancy and Childbirth`, `Psychoneuroendocrinology`, `Social Science & Medicine`, and `Nutrients` directly.
5. Add regional filters for Taiwan, East Asia, Chinese-language validation, or local clinical context.
6. Export citations to Zotero/Mendeley/EndNote.
7. Screen titles and abstracts using prenatal-only inclusion criteria.
8. Separate true clinical diagnosis studies from symptom-scale studies.
9. Extract trimester, instrument, cutoff, comorbid anxiety/PTSD, sample risk level, and treatment status.
10. Build a second-stage search based on gaps found during screening.
